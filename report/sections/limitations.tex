\section{Limitations}

The main limitation of a rule-based detector is that it depends on protocol-specific knowledge and may not generalize automatically to new flash loan implementations. In addition, some protocols expose behavior through internal calls or protocol-specific invariants rather than explicit event names, which makes accurate identification more difficult.

Another limitation is that the detected dataset may not provide complete coverage of all flash loan-like transactions. Therefore, the evaluation should prioritize precision and transparency of rules rather than unsupported claims about full recall.
